Solution - Scenario Author

Team Kiwi collaborated with Western Governors University (WGU) to explore methods to offer engaging, authentic and relevant formative activities to WGU students.

Meet KAT

Anny is an instructional designer. She has been busy with developing communication courses’ formative activities recently. In her past experiences, she finds that students are not engaged in the traditional formative activities. To give students a more engaging learning experience, she decides to create a scenario-based activity with various branches based on students’ input. Nevertheless, she lacks the skill of programming and feels stressed because the deadline to publish this activity for students is fast approaching. Therefore, she made up her mind to find some tools she can use to simply the process of creating scenario based formative activities. We present KAT (Kiwi Authoring Tool) as a solution to target these problems. KAT is an easy to use visual interface that enables curriculum developers to create scenario branches for formative activities by just dragging and dropping blocks and making connections. The blocks cater to the instruction and assessment while the branches that link the various blocks cater to creating scenarios based on student input at every stage of the activity. Users can preview the activity as a student and make necessary changes. They can also share the created activity for others to edit or view. This allows for collaboration in a cross functional team. Anny finds KAT! By dragging the blocks and editing the videos, she creates a constructive feedback activity. The steps followed by her are described in the following pages. After publishing the course, she gets a very positive feedback from students that the activities are engaging. Anny is super happy and feels that the tool is very helpful.

STEP 1: CREATE PROJECT

Anny begins the creation of her new branching scenario activity by creating a new project on the dashboard page. Here, she can also access her prior projects, or projects shared with her if she works in a team.

STEP 2: ADD AND EDIT VIDEO BLOCK

To make the activity engaging and immersive, Anny can add videos to the scenarios. This is done by simply dragging a video block from the sidebar into the canvas. She can also edit the block to upload and trim a video, and drag to make a connection to its previous action or event. New users can get the hang of it quickly by following helpful tooltips.

STEP 3: ADD AND EDIT QUIZ BLOCK

Anny can add student interactions, such as quiz questions, in a similar way. Questions can optionally have video feedback for rich explanations, and be linked with skills to help track students' learning progress.

STEP 4: PREVIEW

At any point in the creation process, Anny can preview the activity from the students' perspective and see if it appears as intended.

STEP 5: SHARE

To have someone review her work or to collaborate, Anny can share the project with others by inviting them using their email addresses. Finally, the activity is complete, and students now have a chance to practice their skills.
